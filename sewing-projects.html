Sewing Projects

I enjoy sewing and mending clothes, particularly using sashiko, which I taught myself. In hope that I can inspire anyone into taking up sewing and mending.

Jeans Knee Repair With Sashiko Pt. 2

July 2025

The original knee repair was bothering me, too undetailed. I decided to redo it, this time using braided fishing line as thread. My biggest and longest project yet. I overcorrected though and went too far the other way, adding so much fine detail that it is only visible up close and just looks like a stain from a distance.

Wallet #2

November 2024

Right after finishing the first wallet I knew I could do better. I used fabric I thrifted and applied what I learned from the first attempt to get the sizing and alignment right. This is by far the project I am most proud of. I have used it every single day for over a year and a half and it is now starting to show signs of patina.

Wallet #1

November 2024

My first attempt at making something from scratch. A bifold wallet sewn together from 3 pieces of fabric. The fabric I chose was too loud and the dimensions came out odd so it never got any real use. Aligning the pieces in a way that made it actually functional was harder than I expected.

Jeans Knee Repair With Sashiko

November 2024

My favourite pair of thrifted jeans worn out with a giant hole in the knee. I backed it with a contrasting black fabric and switched a plus sign pattern over it in sashiko.

Looking back, I spaced the plus signs too far apart because I rushed it.
